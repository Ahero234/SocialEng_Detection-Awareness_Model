Understanding Social Engineering Threats

1. Phishing

Phishing is a cyber attack where attackers impersonate legitimate organizations via email, text messages, or websites to trick individuals into providing sensitive information, such as passwords and credit card details.

How to Identify Phishing:

  • Look for suspicious sender addresses
  • Hover over links before clicking to verify legitimacy
  • Beware of urgent or threatening language in emails

2. Pretexting

Pretexting is a type of social engineering where attackers create a fabricated scenario (pretext) to obtain sensitive information from the target. This often involves impersonating an authority figure, such as IT support or bank representatives.

How to Protect Yourself:

  • Verify the identity of unknown callers before sharing information
  • Avoid disclosing sensitive details over the phone or email
  • Contact the official source directly if in doubt

3. Baiting

Baiting is when attackers use false promises, such as free software, music, or USB devices, to trick victims into downloading malware or revealing personal information.

How to Stay Safe:

  • Never plug in unknown USB drives
  • Avoid downloading software from untrusted sources
  • Be cautious of "too good to be true" offers
